The coding chemist?

As a non-binary person with a PhD in organic chemistry, I had a difficult time trying to figure out how I fit into the academic world. I learned computer modeling of chemistry and honed my teaching skills, but it was still a challenge to find a place where I felt I belonged. I turned to coding after dealing with some severe burnout, requiring more flexibility and creativity in my work.

Now, I specialize in designing and coding websites and other online tools for science research and education. I also take on non-scientific projects that ignite my passion for social justice and environmentalism. I went into science to make the world a better place, and I can now do so on a global scale via web development.
